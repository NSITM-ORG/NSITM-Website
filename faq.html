Frequently Asked Question

Asked Question

Nextserve was founded in 2012, which means we have over fourteen years of experience producing technology practitioners who are ready to work from day one. Most tech training schools in Nigeria have existed for only three to five years – our longevity represents fourteen years of curriculum refinement and understanding what Nigerian employers actually need.

Four things set us apart: (1) Fourteen years of institutional depth, (2) A curriculum built around real-world outcomes instead of theoretical syllabuses, (3) Small cohorts that allow individual attention, and (4) A prerequisite system that puts every student on the right path from day one. We also use real client projects – not practice exercises – so your portfolio shows genuine work.

We offer over twenty-six programmes across three categories: Technology Development (Fullstack Web, Frontend, Backend, Software Engineering, Cybersecurity, Data Analytics, AI/ML, DevOps, Mobile App Dev, UI/UX, Cloud Engineering, Creative Graphics), Management Programmes (Digital Marketing, Project Management, HR, Virtual Assistance, etc.), and Short-Term Professional Programmes (Microsoft Office, AutoCAD, Sage, QuickBook, and more).

Starting in 2026, we moved to a cohort-based delivery model. A cohort is a defined group of students who begin a programme together, learn together, and complete it together. There is a published start date, a fixed schedule, a shared peer group, and a structured accountability system. No student falls behind without the institution noticing. Selected programmes are also delivered fully online with the same quality.

Both. Selected programmes are delivered fully online, extending our reach to students across Nigeria who cannot attend in person. The online cohort experience is designed to deliver the same quality of instruction, peer group dynamic, and practical outcome focus as the in-person experience. For students who prefer in-person learning, our physical facility remains open with morning and afternoon sessions running throughout the year.
