About Us

About Nextserve

Nextserve School of Information Technology and Management is a Nigerian technology training institution with over fourteen years of experience producing practitioners who are ready to work from day one.

We were founded in 2012 on a single conviction: that every Nigerian, regardless of academic background or financial circumstance, deserves access to technology education that actually works. Not education that produces certificate holders. Education that produces people who can build things, solve problems, and contribute meaningfully to the organizations and industries they enter.

That conviction has shaped every decision we have made since we opened our doors, and it remains the foundation of everything we do today.

Our Story

Nextserve began as a focused training centre in a landscape where quality technology education in Nigeria was either unaffordable, inaccessible, or too far removed from the realities of the industry to be genuinely useful.

Over fourteen years, we have grown from a small operation into an institution offering over twenty-six programmes spanning technology development, management, and short-term professional skills. We have trained students from secondary school age through to working professionals in their thirties and forties. We have watched former students go on to employment, freelance careers, and entrepreneurial ventures built entirely on skills they developed within our walls.

We have never relied on marketing campaigns or investor funding to build our reputation. Every student who has come through Nextserve has come because someone who went through Nextserve before them said: this is the place that actually teaches you.

That word-of-mouth reputation, built over more than a decade of genuine outcomes, is the most honest measure of what we are.

Our Founder

Nextserve was founded by a technology educator whose professional formation spans formal technical training at Federal Polytechnic Nekede, industry experience at Synergy Systems, and instructional experience at Yaba College of Technology, one of Nigeria's foremost polytechnics.

That combination, practical industry exposure and professional teaching experience, is what makes Nextserve's approach to technology education distinctive. Our founder did not build a school to fill a market gap. He built it because he understood, from direct experience on both sides of the classroom, what quality technology instruction actually looks like and how rarely it is delivered in Nigeria's private training sector.

His philosophy is straightforward: a student who leaves Nextserve should be able to sit in front of any employer, open their laptop, and show real work. Not a certificate. Not a transcript. Work.



What Makes Us Different

Fourteen years of institutional depth.

Longevity & Experience

Most technology training schools in Nigeria have existed for three to five years. We have been doing this since 2012. That longevity is not just a number. It represents fourteen years of curriculum refinement, fourteen years of understanding what Nigerian employers actually need from their technical hires, and fourteen years of iterating on what works and discarding what does not.

Outcomes, not syllabuses

Our programmes are not designed to teach students what a textbook says about a subject. They are designed to produce students who can do the work. Every module, every session, and every project in our curriculum exists because it produces a capability that matters in the real world. Content that does not meet that standard does not survive our curriculum review process.

Small groups, individual attention

We keep our cohorts deliberately small. Not because we cannot accommodate larger numbers, but because we have learned over fourteen years that the quality of a student's learning experience degrades in direct proportion to how invisible they become in a crowded classroom. Every student at Nextserve is known by name, known by progress, and known by what they are finding difficult.

Prerequisite system

Learning technology is a progression, not a catalogue. A student who enrolls in the wrong programme for their current level does not just struggle, they waste time and money. Before every enrollment, we assess where a prospective student is and make sure they are placed in the programme that will genuinely serve their goals, even if that means recommending a different starting point. That honesty is part of the service.

Real projects, not practice exercises

At the advanced stages of our programmes, students work on real projects for real clients or institutional purposes. They are not simulating professional practice. They are doing it. The portfolio evidence they build through this work is the kind that opens doors in the Nigerian technology job market.

How We Deliver

Cohort-based learning for real accountability

In 2026, Nextserve introduced a cohort-based delivery model across all its technology programmes, replacing the open enrollment walk-in system that had served the institution in its earlier years.

A cohort is a defined group of students who begin a programme together, learn together, and complete it together. There is a published start date, a published schedule, a shared peer group, and a structured accountability system that means no student falls behind without the institution noticing.

Selected programmes are now also delivered fully online, extending Nextserve's reach beyond its physical facility to students across Nigeria who cannot attend in person. The online cohort experience is designed to deliver the same quality of instruction, the same peer group dynamic, and the same practical outcome focus as the in-person experience.

For students who prefer in-person learning, our physical facility remains open and active, with morning and afternoon sessions running throughout the year.

Our Community

More than a school – a network of practitioners

Nextserve is more than a school. It is a community of practitioners, alumni, instructors, and supporters who believe in what technology education can do for Nigeria's workforce and for individual lives.

Our alumni are working as developers, designers, data analysts, project managers, and entrepreneurs across Nigeria and beyond. Some of them have returned to Nextserve as instructors and project supervisors, contributing to the next generation of students the way their own instructors contributed to them.

We are building this community deliberately, through an active alumni network, a referral programme that rewards people for sharing what they believe in, and a growing calendar of events, seminars, and competitions designed to keep the community engaged and connected.

If you are a former student, a prospective student, a parent, or simply someone who believes in what we are doing, you belong in this community. We would be glad to have you.

Where We Are Going

A structured transformation

Nextserve is in the middle of a structured transformation. We are not changing what we fundamentally are. We are building the operational infrastructure, the digital presence, the programme quality systems, and the institutional partnerships that allow the world to see what we have always been.

Over the next three years, we are pursuing formal accreditation with Nigeria's National Board for Technical Education, university and polytechnic partnerships that create structured pathways between academic study and practical skill, an expanded online programme offering that reaches students in every state, and formal recognition as a technology institute in the fullest sense of that designation.

Fourteen years of building the substance. The next three are about making sure the world can see it.
